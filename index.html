Performance and efficiency you can feel

If you want smooth, quiet power with smart efficiency, the INFINITI QX60 stands out. Its 2.0 liter VC-Turbo and 9-speed automatic produce 268 horsepower and make impressive mileage on the 156 or 5 feet combined, which helps on weekend trips to Santa Monica or Duarte. The QX60 can also tow up to 6,000 pounds when properly equipped.

Most Acura MDX trims use a 3.5 liter V6 with 290 horsepower. It is strong, but typical combined mpg trails the QX60. The MDX Type S adds a turbo V6 for more punch, yet it uses more fuel and costs more if efficiency and towing are top priorities, the QX60 makes an easy case.

Comfort, seating, and cargo

Daily comfort is where I see the QX60 connect with families. Available massaging, climate controlled front seats help on longer drives, and heated second row captain's chairs keep everyone happy. Front captain's chairs keep everyone happy. Roof section from quilted semi aniline leather to a panoramic moonroof. Tech, safety, and driver assist

The INFINITI QX60 keeps life simple with an intuitive 12.3 inch touch display, wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, and a Klipsch 20 speaker audio system. The 3D Around View Monitor makes tight parking in Los Angeles stress free. ProPILOT Assist 1.1 supports steering and speed in traffic, and available ProPILOT Assist 2.1 goes even further on select roads.

The MDX brings helpful features of its own, including a full suite of driver assists and available ambient lighting themes. Both SUVs include forward collision warning, automatic emergency braking, and blind spot alerts. For most guests, the QX60 interface feels straightforward and friendly day to day.
